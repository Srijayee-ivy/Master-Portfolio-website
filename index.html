How the Work Was Approached, and What Happened After

Organic Search · Just In Time Case Study 01

Ranking a Luxury Retail Category Page

Context: Wrote the watch-category page for Just In Time's Furla collection, Dec 2022.

Challenge: Differentiate a category page in a market dominated by Myntra, Amazon and Flipkart.

Approach: Structured copy around buyer hesitations — design distinctiveness, precision, durability — not spec order.

Outcome: Ranks #3 on Google today for "Furla watches India buy online," ahead of Amazon.

Content Durability · Khatabook Case Study 02

Writing Fintech Content Built to Survive

Context: Wrote 9+ explainers for Khatabook's blog, 2022–2023, on tax, fraud and accounting.

Challenge: Financial content dates fast; freelance fintech articles usually get replaced within a year.

Approach: Built each piece on a stable definitional structure so numbers could be refreshed without a rewrite.

Outcome: 8 of 9 checked pieces remain live today, several cross-linked across Khatabook's own network.

Local SEO · Tanishq Case Study 03

Researching, Not Templating, Local Pages

Context: Wrote 19 city-specific store-locator pages for Tanishq.

Challenge: Generic "Welcome to [City]" filler is exactly what search engines deprioritize.

Approach: Opened every page with a genuinely researched, city-specific fact, not a reusable paragraph.

Outcome: A repeatable local-SEO framework — Tanishq has since rebuilt this section on a dynamic store-finder, so this is shown as methodology, not a live link.

Operating Process

How I Run a Project, Start to Finish

Not just "I can write it" — this is the actual sequence, including the QA habits from Amazon and the audit habit that built the catalogue below.

01

Brief & Brand Voice Intake

Read the brief against existing brand-voice and style guidelines first — garment-attribute and tone rules per client, before a single line got written.

02

Structure Before Sentences

Map search intent or buyer hesitation points before drafting — the reasoning behind the Furla and Khatabook case studies above.

03

Draft in Brand Voice

Write to the structure, not around it — keeping format-specific constraints (SEO, UX, landing copy) and brand tone in the same draft, not reconciled after the fact.

04

QA at Scale

At Amazon's Alexa team, this meant evaluating content against evolving guidelines and feeding recurring error patterns back to the team — the same instinct applied to every batch of work here.

05

Publish, Then Audit

Delivery isn't the finish line — checking what actually went live, and what happened to it months or years later, is what built the verification system below.
